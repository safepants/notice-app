Deep Conversation Topics

These aren't debate topics. They're doors. Each one opens into something you've probably thought about but rarely talked about out loud. Best shared with someone you trust — or someone you want to trust you.

  1. 1What do you think happens when we die?
  2. 2If this is as good as it gets, would that be enough?
  3. 3What's one thing about love not enough people notice?
  4. 4What aspect of society do you reject?
  5. 5What is one reason to be optimistic about the world today?
  6. 6If you could get the whole world behind one idea, what would it be?
  7. 7Name a belief you held a year ago that you've quietly dropped
  8. 8How do people in your life help you be a better person?
  9. 9What do you think your life looks like from the outside?
  10. 10What's something you know is true that most people don't believe?
  11. 11Say something honest right now. About anything
  12. 12"Most people feel more alone now than they did 10 years ago." Address the statement like a politician
  13. 13What's a time you were brave? Tell the group, even if you don't feel like a hero
  14. 14What's something you've never said to anyone in this room?
  15. 15What's one of the most underrated qualities in life?
  16. 16If you could have a conversation with any version of yourself — past or future — which one?
  17. 17What would a stranger think if they walked in right now?
  18. 18Will you live a great life?

frequently asked

How do you start a deep conversation?

You don't announce it. You just ask a better question. 'What's on your mind these days?' is a better opening than 'Let's have a deep talk.' The depth comes from the question, not from declaring your intention.

What if someone isn't ready for a deep conversation?

Then they'll give a surface answer, and that's fine. Don't push. Sometimes someone needs to hear a deep question three times before they're ready to answer it honestly. Just asking it plants the seed.
