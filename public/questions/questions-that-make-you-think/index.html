Questions That Make You Think

These questions don't have right answers. They have your answers. Each one asks you to notice something you might have missed — about yourself, about the room, about the way things are. Best shared, but worth sitting with alone too.

  1. 1If you could ask the entire world one question and get an honest answer, what would you ask?
  2. 2Look at your hands. What's the most recent thing they did that mattered?
  3. 3If your life was a movie, what is the audience chanting for you to do right now?
  4. 4What do you think your life looks like from the outside?
  5. 5If you could see one measurement or statistic over everyone's heads, what would you want it to indicate?
  6. 6What would a stranger think if they walked in right now?
  7. 7If everyone in this room had met in a different lifetime, what do you think the setting would be?
  8. 8What's the oldest thing in this room?
  9. 9Right now — what are you most aware of that you weren't aware of when this game started?
  10. 10Name something that exists right now that would blow someone's mind 200 years ago
  11. 11What would you do with an extra hour every day that nobody knew about?
  12. 12If you could witness any moment in history — just watch, not change it — what would you pick?
  13. 13What's a place that changed the way you see things?
  14. 14What sound can you hear right now that you weren't aware of 5 seconds ago?
  15. 15Name a belief you held a year ago that you've quietly dropped
  16. 16What's one thing about this room you'll remember a week from now?
  17. 17What do you notice about the place you're in?
  18. 18What's the furthest thing you can see from where you're sitting?

frequently asked

Why do some questions make you think more than others?

The best ones reframe something familiar. 'What's the oldest thing in this room?' makes you see a space you've been in a hundred times with completely fresh eyes. It's not about complexity — it's about shifting perspective.

Can you use thought-provoking questions in everyday conversation?

That's the whole point. You don't need a philosophy seminar. 'What sound can you hear right now that you weren't aware of 5 seconds ago?' works in literally any setting. The best thought-provoking questions fit into normal life.
