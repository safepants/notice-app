Fun Questions to Ask Anyone

Not every question needs to be deep. Sometimes you just need something that makes people laugh, argue, or reveal something absurd about themselves. These are the ones that always land — light enough for anywhere, interesting enough to keep going.

  1. 1Everyone pick a number from 1 to 100. See who picked closest to each other
  2. 2If animals could talk, which one would you hear from first?
  3. 3Are you more of a hehe or haha type of person?
  4. 4If months were people, which one would be the rudest?
  5. 5What was your childhood nickname?
  6. 6If this group started a band, what would it be called?
  7. 7If they made a movie about your life, what would it be called?
  8. 8Point at the person who would survive the longest in the wild. Explain
  9. 9If you had to send one person in this room to represent the group to aliens, who and why?
  10. 10Everyone point at the funniest person in the room on the count of 3. 1, 2, 3
  11. 11Close your eyes. Point to where you think north is. Open. Check. If correct, congratulations — you know North!
  12. 12Show your lock screen. Why that image?
  13. 13Show the group your screen time for today
  14. 14What's the most unhinged thing you've ever done that you'd do again?
  15. 15What's something you believed way too long as a kid?
  16. 16What is a time you got in a lot of trouble?
  17. 17One meal, free and zero calories except the good ones — what is it?
  18. 18What song do you think they're playing in heaven right now? Queue the music

frequently asked

What's a fun question to ask in a group?

The ones where everyone has to participate. 'Everyone point at the funniest person on 3' or 'Everyone guess what time it is without looking' — these create a shared moment, not just a Q&A.

How do you keep a conversation fun without it being shallow?

Mix light and real. Start with something absurd ('If months were people, which one is the rudest?') and let it naturally lead somewhere. The best conversations oscillate between funny and honest.
